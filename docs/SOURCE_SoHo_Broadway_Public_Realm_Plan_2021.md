## What they did (summary)

1. **Scope:** Public-realm vision for **Broadway (Houston–Canal)** plus cross streets (Prince, Spring, Broome, Grand, Howard) and expansion to **Crosby** and **Mercer**—overlaps your **Houston–Canal** study geography closely.

2. **Process (Dec 2020–Oct 2021):** 300+ stakeholders; intercept surveys; digital survey; focus groups; **Public Realm Task Force** (includes **NYC DOT** Manhattan Borough Commissioner, electeds, property/retail/residents); ties to **Envision SoHo/NoHo** (DCP / Brewer / Chin).

3. **Diagnosis:** Static public realm strained by mixed-use density; congestion, noise, air pollution; narrow sidewalks; crowding; freight/service/logistics stress; **Holland Tunnel regional traffic** through the district.

4. **Explicit “LTN” framing:** The plan names a **“Low-Traffic Neighborhood: A Design + Mobility Framework”** and ties it to **diverting non-local traffic**, **modal filters / diverters**, road diets, enforcement, etc., plus larger **SoHo/Nolita/Little Italy/NW Chinatown** as a contiguous LTN aligned with **Broadway linear public space** history (RPA 1969 → DOT projects since 2009).

5. **Quantified goals (examples):** Widen sidewalks **≥50%**; **pedestrian-priority / car-free** Prince & Howard; **500%** more on-street seating; **divert** non-local trips; **~66%** reduction goal for regional traffic on **Broadway and Broome**; **75%** crash reduction goal; major **tree canopy** increase; **Broadway** as **curbless** linear space; **non-local traffic** to perimeter “superblock” (Houston–Bowery–Varick–Canal).

6. **Delivery model:** **20-year** horizon; **Key Moves** (capital) + **Strategic Initiatives** + **Tactical** demos + **Supporting Actions**; stresses **pilots**, interim materials, and **DOT / DDC / EDC** roles.
