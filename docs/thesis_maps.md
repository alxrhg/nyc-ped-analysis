# Thesis maps (Manhattan): walking, crashes, residential unit mix, subway access

Generated by `scripts/make_manhattan_maps.py` into `outputs/maps/`.

## What the maps show

1. **`01_pedestrian_intensity_percentile.png`**  
   **Borough percentile** of a pedestrian **exposure proxy per km¬≤** by **2020 census tract** (default):  
   \(\text{ped\_density}_i = \mathrm{ped\_metric}_i / \mathrm{area}^{2263}_i\) with \(\mathrm{ped\_metric}_i\) built from MIT NYCWalks segments. **Default exposure:** for each peak-period column (`predwkdyAM/MD/PM`, `predwkndAM/MD/PM`) the code sums **\(\sum \widehat{\mathrm{vol}}_\ell \cdot \mathrm{len}^{2263}_{\ell\cap i}\)** using **line‚Äìtract intersection** in EPSG:2263 (only the length **inside** tract \(i\) counts). **`ped_metric`** is the **arithmetic mean** of those six tract totals (missing columns dropped). **Legacy / sensitivity:** `--ped-metric-source single --volume-col ‚Ä¶` uses one column only; `--ped-spatial-aggregation centroid` assigns each segment‚Äôs **full** vol√ólength to the tract of the segment centroid.  
   **Trip-purpose splits:** numeric **`HME_*`** columns in the GeoJSON are aggregated the same way and saved as **`nw_exp_HME_*`** (not averaged into `ped_metric` unless you choose one with `--volume-col`). Use **`--ped-extra-maps purpose`** (or `all`) for separate maps. See `outputs/maps/nycwalks_aggregation_summary.txt` for the column list on your file.  
   **Legacy:** `--ped-percentile-basis total` ranks the **raw tract sum** instead of per km¬≤ (Central Park can dominate).  
   - **Preferred input:** MIT [NYCWalks](https://cityform.mit.edu/projects/nycwalks) GeoJSON (e.g. `NYC_pednetwork_estimates_counts_2018-2019.geojson`). Symlink into `data/mit/` (see `data/README.md`).  
   - **Fallback:** NYC DOT *Bi-Annual Pedestrian Counts* (~114 fixed survey locations). Many tracts have **no** survey point, so lots of tracts tie at low values‚Äî**use NYCWalks for a defensible map.**

2. **`02_crash_intensity_percentile.png`**  
   **Borough percentile** of **crash count per tract km¬≤** (default), same tract area basis as map 1 (EPSG:2263 area ‚Üí km¬≤): \(\text{crash\_density}_i = \mathrm{crash\_count}_i / \mathrm{area}^{2263}_i\). **Legacy:** `--crash-percentile-basis total` ranks raw counts. Data: geocoded **NYPD motor vehicle crashes** ([Motor Vehicle Collisions](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/d/h9gi-nx95)) joined to tracts. Supports **where crash activity is dense** relative to tract size (e.g. corridors vs large low-count tracts).

3. **`03_residential_intensity_percentile.png`**  
   **Borough percentile** of the **share of PLUTO building units that are residential** in each tract:

   \[
   s_i = 100 \times \frac{\sum_{\text{lots } \ell \in i} \mathrm{UnitsRes}_\ell}{\sum_{\ell \in i} \mathrm{UnitsTotal}_\ell}
   \]

   Data: NYC DCP **[PLUTO](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/d/64uk-42ks)** (tabular `64uk-42ks`), lots keyed to **2020 census tract** via `bct2020` ‚Üí borough tract ‚Üí `geoid` (`36061‚Ä¶`). **`UnitsTotal`** is all units attributed to the tax lot‚Äôs buildings; **`UnitsRes`** is the residential portion. Everything else (office, retail, institutional, etc.) is in the **non-residential remainder**‚ÄîPLUTO does **not** split ‚Äúbusiness‚Äù vs ‚Äúother‚Äù subtypes at the unit level. Tracts with **no** reported units (denominator 0) are left **missing** on the map.

   **ACS** `B25001` / km¬≤ is still merged for reference as **`acs_hu_per_km2`** in `study_area_tract_rankings.csv` if you want density language in prose.

4. **`04_combined_multimetric_index.png`**  
   **Single choropleth** of a **combined index** where **pedestrian volume** is primary, a **pedestrian harm‚Äìexposure** term is secondary, and **residential share** is a small tie-breaker (unless you change balance). **Maps 1‚Äì3** stay **borough-wide** percentiles; **map 4** uses its own inputs (below).

   **Pedestrian KSI rate (default crash leg):** Let \(H_i\) be the sum over the crash window of **pedestrians injured + killed** (NYPD `h9gi-nx95`) in tract \(i\), and \(E_i\) the NYCWalks exposure proxy (`ped_metric`). Then  
   \(\text{ksi\_rate}_i = H_i / (E_i + 1)\).  
   **\(K_i\)** is the **percentile rank of \(\text{ksi\_rate}_i\)** among tracts in the chosen **scope**. `--combined-crash-metric count` uses the **same basis as map 2** (default **crashes per km¬≤**; `--crash-percentile-basis total` for raw counts), still scope-filtered for map 4.

   **Geographic scope for map 4 (default):** `--combined-scope borough` ranks \(P\), \(K\) (or \(C\)), and (if used) \(s\) among **all** Manhattan census tracts so **Upper Manhattan is included** on the combined map. **Optional:** `--combined-scope south_of` ranks **only** tracts whose centroid lies **south** of `--combined-scope-north-lat` (default **40.765**, ~59th St band); tracts **north** of that line are **missing** on map 4 (light grey)‚Äîuseful for a downtown-only sensitivity figure.

   **Default mean** (`--combined-method mean`, `--combined-res raw_share`, `--combined-balance ped_primary`):

   \[
   I_i = 0.65\,P_i + 0.30\,K_i + 0.05\,s_i
   \]

   Here \(P_i\) is the **scope** percentile of the same pedestrian basis as map 1 (default **per km¬≤**; `--ped-percentile-basis total` matches legacy tract totals), recomputed within the map‚Äë4 pool. Map (4) uses **`Greys`**: low index = light, **high = dark**.

   **Ped + harm only:** `--combined-balance ped_crash` ‚Üí \(I_i = 0.55\,P_i + 0.45\,K_i\) (no \(s_i\)).

   **Equal thirds:** `--combined-balance equal` ‚Üí \(\frac{1}{3}(P_i + K_i + s_i)\) (or \(C_i\) if `count`).

   **Legacy:** `--combined-balance ped_emphasis` ‚Üí \(\tfrac{1}{2}P_i+\tfrac{1}{4}K_i+\tfrac{1}{4}s_i\). `--combined-res percentile` uses \(R_i\) instead of \(s_i\).

   **Optional** (`--combined-method geometric`): three-factor cube root on \(P\), \(K\) (or \(C\)), and \(s\) or \(R\); with `ped_crash`, \(I = 100\sqrt{(P/100)(K/100)}\).

   **Study-area emphasis (optional):** `--study-area-weight W` (0‚Äì1) blends the combined index toward a **binary ‚Äúin study polygon‚Äù** score so the **CSCL quadrilateral** (SoHo / Chinatown corridor) can read darker than FiDi/Midtown without hiding the rest of the borough:  
   \(I_{\text{final}} = (1-W)\,I_{\text{base}} + W\,G\) with \(G=100\) if the tract **intersects** `study_area_bowery_houston_6th_canal.geojson`, else \(G=0\). **`combined_idx_base`** in the CSV is the value **before** this blend (and **before** the rank floor below). Try **W ‚âà 0.10‚Äì0.15** for a visible lift; justify in the thesis as an explicit **policy / analysis focus** weighting.

   **Study tract rank floor (default on):** `--study-floor-at-borough-rank K` (default **K = 3**) runs **after** the optional study blend. Every tract that **intersects** the study polygon gets  
   \(I \leftarrow \max(I,\, I_{(K)})\),  
   where \(I_{(K)}\) is the **K-th largest** combined index among **all** Manhattan tracts (same values as on map 4, borough-wide). That keeps the corridor in the **top tier** of the choropleth‚Äîso it does not **blend into** the mid-range of the island‚Äî**without** asserting that every corridor tract ‚Äúbeats‚Äù the strongest Midtown/FiDi tracts. Use **`--study-floor-at-borough-rank 0`** for a run with **no** floor (pure index only). Requires the study GeoJSON; if the polygon is missing, the floor is skipped.

   `study_area_tract_rankings.csv` includes **`combined_idx`**, **`combined_idx_base`**, **`study_intersects`**, **`study_floor_threshold`** (borough \(I_{(K)}\) when the floor is on, else NaN), **`combined_ped_pctile`**, **`combined_crash_leg_pctile`**, **`ped_ksi`**, **`ksi_rate`**, **`combined_scope_in`**, **`dist_nearest_subway_m`**, **`subway_access_pctile`**, **`nearest_subway_ridership_sum`**, **`subway_nearest_ridership_pctile`**, **`transit_access_ridership_pctile`**, and is sorted by **`combined_idx`** (highest first).

5. **`05_subway_access_percentile.png`** (when subway step succeeds)  
   **Subway proximity:** for each tract, **planar distance in meters** (EPSG:2263) from the **tract polygon** to the nearest **MTA subway station stop point** in **Manhattan** (`borough = M`). Stops come from NY Open Data **[MTA Subway Stations](https://data.ny.gov/Transportation/MTA-Subway-Stations/39hk-dx4f)** (`39hk-dx4f` on **data.ny.gov**). **`subway_access_pctile`** is a **Manhattan borough percentile** where **higher = closer** to a stop (via `max(d)-d` before ranking, same convention as other 0‚Äì100 layers). This is **straight-line** distance in the projected CRS, **not** network walking time. **Offline / skip:** `--skip-subway-distance`.

6. **`06_transit_access_ridership_percentile.png`** (when ridership loads)  
   Combines **distance to the nearest stop** with **how busy that stop‚Äôs complex is**. For each tract, \(R\) = **sum of MTA-reported monthly ridership (entries)** at the **nearest stop‚Äôs** `complex_id`, over all Manhattan rows with `month ‚â•` **`--subway-ridership-since`** in **[MTA Subway Station Monthly Ridership](https://data.ny.gov/Transportation/MTA-Subway-Station-Monthly-Ridership-Beginning-February/ak4z-sape)** (`ak4z-sape`, joined on `station_complex_id` ‚Üî stops `complex_id`). \(d\) = planar distance (m) as in map 5. **Score** \(= \log(1+R)/(d + b)\) with buffer \(b=\) **`--transit-gravity-buffer-m`** (default 50 m); **`transit_access_ridership_pctile`** is its **Manhattan borough percentile** (higher = nearer a **high-ridership** complex, not just any stop). **`subway_nearest_ridership_pctile`** ranks **only** \(\log(1+R)\) at the nearest complex (distance ignored). **Skip ridership only:** `--skip-subway-ridership` (still builds map 5).

## Neighborhood borders (NTA roll-up, optional)

For **general NYC ‚Äúneighborhood‚Äù units** that are still **official** boundaries, use **2020 Neighborhood Tabulation Areas (NTAs)** from NYC Open Data ([**9nt8-h7nd**](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/d/9nt8-h7nd)): DCP/Census polygons (names like *SoHo‚ÄìLittle Italy‚ÄìHudson Square*, *Chinatown*, *Turtle Bay*, etc.), **not** informal hand-drawn neighborhoods.

1. Export tract metrics:  
   `python scripts/make_manhattan_maps.py --write-tract-metrics outputs/maps/manhattan_tract_metrics.gpkg`  
   (same combined index / percentiles as map 4 for that run).

2. Build the NTA table:  
   `python scripts/rank_manhattan_nta.py`  
   ‚Üí **`outputs/maps/manhattan_nta_rankings.csv`**: each Manhattan NTA gets **area-weighted** means of tract metrics (each tract assigned to the **single NTA** with the **largest planar overlap**), plus **percentiles among Manhattan NTAs** (e.g. **`combined_idx_nta_pctile`**). If tract metrics include subway columns, the CSV also has **`dist_nearest_subway_m_awm`**, **`subway_access_pctile_awm`**, and related NTA-level percentiles. Optional choropleth: e.g. `--out-map outputs/maps/07_combined_index_nta.png` (use **07+** so filenames do not collide with **`05_subway_access_percentile.png`** / **`06_transit_access_ridership_percentile.png`**).

This is a **different geographic scale** than census tracts: good for readable labels and policy narrative; tracts remain the finer analysis unit.

## Should you discuss the combined index in the thesis?

**Yes, briefly.** The weighted mean (and optional geometric alternative) is a **transparent, arbitrary simplification**. State (1) **\(P\)** and **\(K\)** (KSI per exposure, or crash counts if you switch) for **map 4** use the chosen **geographic scope** (default **all Manhattan**; `south_of` only if you use that sensitivity run); **\(s\)** is minor unless you equal-weight; (2) maps 1‚Äì3 remain borough-wide for context; (3) if you keep the default **rank floor**, name it as an explicit **visibility rule** for the study polygon (not a claim that the corridor outranks every core CBD tract); (4) limitations: tract scale, PLUTO, NYCWalks, and **fast** crash downloads truncating \(H_i\). Compare `south_of` scope, `count` metric, balances, `--combined-res percentile`, `--study-floor-at-borough-rank 0`, or `--combined-method geometric` for sensitivity.

**Study outline (crimson):** Quadrilateral from **NYC Street Centerline** (`inkn-q76z`): **Avenue of the Americas** (west), **Houston St** (W + E segments, north), **Bowery** (east), **Canal St** (south, excluding Holland Tunnel label). Saved as `data/mit/study_area_bowery_houston_6th_canal.geojson`. Rebuild with `python scripts/build_study_area_polygon.py` if DCP updates CSCL.

## Why SoHo / Chinatown for the corridor

Use the **three percentile maps** together for interpretation, or the **combined map (4)** for a single summary surface; the equation documents how (4) relates to (1‚Äì3).

Use the maps **together**, not in isolation:

- **Chinatown‚ÄìTwo Bridges** and nearby tracts often show **very high crash percentiles** (see `study_area_tract_rankings.csv`)‚Äîconsistent with **heavy walking √ó motor traffic** in a tight grid.
- **Residential unit share** (map 3) can stay **high** where PLUTO reports many dwelling units per total units; **SoHo-style** tracts with large commercial footprints may show **lower** \(s_i\) than residential-dominated tracts‚Äîcheck **`res_unit_share_pct`** and **`acs_hu_per_km2`** in the CSV.
- **Pedestrian intensity** (map 1) uses **exposure per tract km¬≤** by default so rankings are closer to ‚Äúdensity‚Äù than raw totals inside huge tracts; use `--ped-percentile-basis total` only if you intentionally want Œ£(vol√ólength) without area normalization.

## Methods one-liner for the thesis

‚ÄúTract-level maps use NYC Open Data 2020 tract boundaries ([63ge-mke6](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/d/63ge-mke6)), NYPD geocoded crashes **per tract km¬≤**, PLUTO tax-lot unit totals for **residential share of building units**, ACS 2022 5-year counts for optional density context, MIT NYCWalks segment volumes √ó length **normalized by tract area (km¬≤)** for the walking layer, optional **planar distance to the nearest MTA Manhattan subway stop** ([39hk-dx4f](https://data.ny.gov/Transportation/MTA-Subway-Stations/39hk-dx4f)), and optional **summed MTA station ridership** at that stop‚Äôs complex ([ak4z-sape](https://data.ny.gov/Transportation/MTA-Subway-Station-Monthly-Ridership-Beginning-February/ak4z-sape)) combined with distance as \(\log(1+R)/(d+b)\); tract metrics are **Manhattan percentiles** unless noted.‚Äù
